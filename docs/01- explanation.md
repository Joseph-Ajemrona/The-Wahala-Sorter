1. The app starts with a power switch called `main.jsx` that tells the computer to show our board on the screen.

2. We have three starting chores already on the board so it doesn't look empty when we open it for the first time.

3. The app has a memory box that remembers the chores you typed and whether you like the screen bright like the sun or dark like the night.

4. When we type a chore name and select a box, clicking the button makes a new chore sticker with a clock time showing when you made it.

5. You can hold a chore sticker with your mouse or finger, drag it through the air, and drop it into another box, and the board remembers exactly where you put it.

6. The screen automatically updates whenever you add, delete, or move a chore, and also saves everything to the browser's secret memory box so your chores don't disappear when you close the tab.
